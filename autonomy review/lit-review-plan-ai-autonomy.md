# Literature Review Plan: AI's Impact on Workplace Autonomy

**Research Topic**: Examining how AI impacts workplace autonomy and whether existing philosophical/social-scientific accounts of autonomy need revision

**Research Approach**: Empirically-informed conceptual analysis using qualitative interviews with employees

**Date**: November 17, 2025

---

## Research Objectives

This literature review supports a research project that:
1. Uses qualitative interviews with employees about workplace autonomy and AI's impact
2. Employs these empirical findings as input for theorizing/revising the concept of autonomy
3. Integrates philosophical and social-scientific perspectives
4. Identifies gaps in current autonomy theories for understanding AI-mediated work contexts

---

## Domain Structure

### Domain 1: Philosophical Perspectives on Autonomy
**Scope**: Both general autonomy theory AND philosophical work on autonomy at work

**Key Sub-domains**:
- Classical philosophical accounts (Kant, Mill, Frankfurt, Dworkin)
- Contemporary autonomy theory (relational autonomy, procedural vs substantive, competency accounts)
- Recent debates about revising autonomy concepts
- Philosophical work on autonomy in work contexts and labor
- Applied ethics perspectives on workplace autonomy
- Freedom, agency, and self-determination in philosophical literature

**Key Questions**:
- What are the dominant theoretical frameworks for understanding autonomy in philosophy?
- How do procedural vs substantive accounts differ?
- What is the relational autonomy critique?
- How do philosophers conceptualize autonomy specifically in work contexts?
- What are recent debates about revising or updating autonomy concepts?

**Search Strategy**:
- Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy (SEP) entries on autonomy, freedom, agency
- PhilPapers searches: "autonomy theory", "workplace autonomy philosophy", "autonomy at work", "labor autonomy", "relational autonomy"
- Google Scholar: foundational texts (Frankfurt, Dworkin, Christman) + recent work (2015-2025)
- Key journals: *Philosophy & Public Affairs*, *Ethics*, *Journal of Political Philosophy*, *Social Theory and Practice*
- Applied ethics journals with work focus

**Expected Papers**: 30-40 (foundational + contemporary + work-specific)

**Key Questions**:

**Search Strategy**:

**Expected Papers**: 35-45 (general + workplace-specific + critical perspectives)
