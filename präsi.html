The Big Question

Can institutional cultures shape epistemic character in adulthood?

  • The Skeptics:
    • Aristotle: Character is settled early.
    • Situationists: Character is a myth; context is everything.
  • The Stakes: Democratic Resilience.
    • Policing requires Democratic Epistemic Virtues.
    • If we can't shape character, we can't steward democracy.

The Thesis

Epistemic character is malleable, but the mechanism is Interactionist.

  1. Ontology: Institutions act as Epistemic Scaffolding (Non-Summativism).
  2. Development: Agents exercise Critical Disenchantment (Reactive Agency).
  3. Politics: Epistemic culture protects democracy through individual virtue.

What is "Epistemic Culture"?

The shared norms, practices, and values concerning knowledge, inquiry, and error.

Handling Dissent

Is disagreement punished as insubordination or valued as a safety check?

Error Culture

Is "I don't know" a fireable offense or a starting point for inquiry?

External Expertise

Is citizen testimony dismissed? (Testimonial Injustice)

The Ontological Debate

  • Summativism:
    Group Virtue = Sum of Member Virtues.
    "A culture of humility is just a lot of humble people."
  • Non-Summativism (My View):
    Groups have irreducible epistemic properties.
    "The culture acts as a structure that changes the people inside it."

The Test: Does perceived culture predict change in members?

The Laboratory: German Police Education

  • Setting: Police Academy in NRW, Germany.
  • Design: 3-Wave Longitudinal Panel.
    • Wave 1: Start of studies.
    • Wave 2: One year in.
    • Wave 3: Two years in.
  • Sample: $N=200$ recruits (Completed all waves).
Why this matters: Tracking the same people allows us to control for baseline character. We aren't just seeing selection effects.

Collective Epistemic Stewardship

  • The Duty: Organizations must sustain healthy epistemic cultures.
  • The Danger of Disenchantment:
    • Relying on "Lukas" is risky. It is cognitively costly (Burdened Virtue).
    • Many recruits will simply become cynical, not virtuous.
  • The Goal: Design for Mia, but leave space for Lukas.

Conclusion

  1. Elias was partially right: Baseline character is stable.
  2. Mia shows us: Institutions do scaffold virtue (Non-Summativism is real).
  3. Lukas shows us: Agents are not passive; failure can catalyze growth.

The "Blue Mind" is not fixed.
It is shaped by the collective.
