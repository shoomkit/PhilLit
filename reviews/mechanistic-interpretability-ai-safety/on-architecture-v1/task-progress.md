## Key Findings

1. **Definitional Pluralism**: Hendrycks & Hiscott (narrow MI) vs. Kästner & Crook (broad MI) are not contradictory but talking about different things
2. **Qualified Necessity**: MI may be necessary for deception detection and inner alignment verification, but not for all safety properties
3. **Clear Non-Sufficiency**: MI is demonstrably not sufficient for AI safety due to scalability limits, understanding-control gap, and missing safety components
4. **Practical Recommendations**: Methodological pluralism, not MI-only approaches; resource diversification across safety methods
5. **Philosophical Value**: Conceptual analysis dissolves apparent empirical contradictions and clarifies productive research directions
