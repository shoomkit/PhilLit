## Key Research Findings (from completed literature search)

### Definitional Pluralism Confirmed:
- **Narrow MI** (Hendrycks & Hiscott 2025): Neuron-level activations
- **Broad MI** (Kästner & Crook 2024): Functional mechanistic explanations
- **Technical diversity**: Circuits, sparse autoencoders, probing, feature visualization

### AI Safety Landscape:
- Deception emerging: Alignment faking (Anthropic 2024), strategic deception (GPT-4, o1)
- Alternative safety approaches: Constitutional AI, formal verification, scalable oversight
- Interpretability one of multiple safety research directions

### Philosophical Frameworks Available:
- Mechanistic explanation (Machamer-Darden-Craver, Craver 2007, Bechtel 2010)
- Levels of analysis (Marr 1982)
- Constitutive vs. etiological explanation (Siegel & Craver 2024)
- Epistemic opacity (Carabantes 2020, Zednik 2021)

### Empirical Evidence Mixed:
- **MI successes**: Anthropic scaling monosemanticity found safety-relevant features
- **MI challenges**: DeepMind deprioritized SAEs, scalability issues documented
- **Alternative successes**: Constitutional AI, formal verification progress
- **Remaining problems**: Alignment faking despite safety training
